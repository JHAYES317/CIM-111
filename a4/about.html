About

17 miles northeast of Miami, Aventura ( Adventure in Spanish) is a city in Miami-Dade County, Florida. It was first called Turnberry, first being developed in the 1970s before finally becoming part of the county in 1995.

Education

The main public school of Aventura is the Aventura Waterways K-8 center. In addition, there is a K-8 charter school called the Aventura City of Excellence School operated by private company Charter Schools USA, which at one point was in the top 10 of Florida schools. There is only one high school serving Aventura called Dr. Michael Krop High School, which is a magnet school part of the STAR program, but with a rising population, the Don Soffer Aventura High School is being built set to open in late 2019.

Media

Aventura News is the main newspaper of Aventura, part of Miami's The Voice of the Community program. It has no internal radio station, and is served by the Miami-Ft. Lauderdale station for programming. Aventura Magazine is the magazine provider for Aventura and is sponsored by the Miami Herald

Climate

Aventura has a wet season in the summer and a dry season in winter, given that it has a tropical monsoon climate. In the summer it is very humid with temperatures usually ranging from 75 degrees fahrenheit to 90 degrees fahrenheit due to the Gulf Stream cycle. In winter, there is less humidity and temperatures can drop down to 40 degrees fahrenheit and don't exceed 75 degrees fahrenheit.

Transportation

Aventura has a public bus service called the Aventura Express, which is free to residents, and also sponsors the Miami-Dade Transit and Broward County Bus Transit services as well. Due to traffic congestion, there were renovations to Biscayne Boulevard to promote travel towards Interstate 95, but the remaining roads were unchanged and thus there still remains heavy traffic.
